What is a QR Code?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a type of matrix barcode that can store various types of information. Originally developed in Japan for tracking automotive parts, QR codes have become widely used for sharing information quickly and efficiently.

QR codes can store up to 4,296 alphanumeric characters and can be read by smartphones, tablets, and dedicated QR code scanners. They're perfect for:

  • Sharing website URLs
  • Distributing contact information
  • Providing WiFi access
  • Creating email links
  • Sending SMS messages
  • Storing plain text

Learn more about QR codes on Wikipedia.

WiFi QR Codes

WiFi QR codes allow users to connect to wireless networks automatically without manually entering credentials. Perfect for:

  • Guest networks
  • Cafes and restaurants
  • Offices and coworking spaces
  • Events and conferences

How to Create a WiFi QR Code:

  1. Select the "WiFi" tab
  2. Enter the network name (SSID)
  3. Enter the password (if required)
  4. Select the security type (WPA/WPA2, WEP, or No Password)
  5. Check "Hidden Network" if applicable
  6. Click "Generate QR Code"
  7. Download your QR code

Security Note: Be cautious when sharing WiFi QR codes as they contain your network password.

Best Practices

Design and Placement

  • Size matters: Ensure QR codes are large enough to scan (minimum 2x2 cm)
  • High contrast: Use dark QR codes on light backgrounds
  • Quiet zone: Leave white space around the QR code
  • Test before printing: Always test your QR codes before mass production

Content Guidelines

  • Keep URLs short: Long URLs create complex QR codes that are harder to scan
  • Verify information: Double-check all entered data for accuracy
  • Consider mobile users: Ensure linked content is mobile-friendly
  • Provide context: Include instructions or descriptions near your QR codes

Security Considerations

  • WiFi passwords: Only share WiFi QR codes with trusted individuals
  • URL verification: Ensure URLs lead to legitimate, safe websites
  • Contact information: Only include information you're comfortable sharing publicly

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this QR code generator free?

Yes, our QR code generator is completely free to use. No registration, payment, or subscription required.

What image format are the QR codes?

All QR codes are generated as high-quality PNG images, perfect for both digital and print use.

How long do the QR codes last?

QR codes generated with our tool are static and will work indefinitely. However, if you're linking to external content (like websites), ensure those resources remain available.

Can I customize the appearance of QR codes?

Currently, our generator creates standard black and white QR codes optimized for maximum compatibility and scanning reliability.

What's the maximum amount of data I can store?

The data capacity depends on the type of content:

  • Text: Up to 1,000 characters
  • URLs: Practically unlimited (though shorter URLs scan better)
  • Contact info: All standard vCard fields supported
  • SMS: Up to 160 characters for the message

Do QR codes work on all devices?

Yes, QR codes can be scanned by any device with a camera and QR code scanning capability, including smartphones, tablets, and dedicated scanners.

Can I use these QR codes commercially?

Absolutely! You can use the generated QR codes for any purpose, including commercial applications, without any restrictions.

How do I scan a QR code?

Most modern smartphones have built-in QR code scanning in their camera apps. Simply open your camera and point it at the QR code. You can also download dedicated QR code scanner apps from your device's app store.

Why isn't my QR code working?

Common issues include:

  • QR code is too small or blurry
  • Poor lighting conditions
  • Damaged or distorted QR code
  • Incorrect URL format (missing http:// or https://)
  • Linked content is no longer available
